Akara

Description

A popular West African street food made from blended black-eyed peas, deep fried into fluffy golden fritters.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups black-eyed peas
  • 1 pieces onion
  • 2 pieces scotch bonnet pepper
  • 1 teaspoons salt
  • 2.1 cups vegetable oil
  • 4 tablespoons water

Steps

  1. Peel the beans: Soak 2 cups black-eyed peas in water for 30 minutes. Rub between your palms to remove the skins. Rinse until all skins are removed.
  2. Blend: Blend peeled beans with 1 pieces onion, 2 pieces scotch bonnet pepper and 4 tablespoons water into a smooth thick paste.
  3. Whisk: Transfer paste to a bowl and whisk vigorously for 5 minutes to incorporate air — this makes the akara fluffy. Add 1 teaspoons salt and mix well.
  4. Fry: Heat 2.1 cups vegetable oil in a deep pan over medium heat. Scoop batter with a spoon and drop carefully into hot oil. Fry in batches for 4 minutes per side until golden brown.
  5. Drain and serve: Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels. Serve hot with pap (akamu) or bread.
