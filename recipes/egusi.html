Egusi

Description

A hearty and delicious Nigerian soup made from ground melon seeds, packed with assorted meats and leafy vegetables.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups ground egusi (melon seeds)
  • 17.9 ounces assorted meat (beef, tripe, shaki)
  • 3.6 ounces stockfish
  • 4 tablespoons palm oil
  • 1 pieces onion
  • 3 pieces scotch bonnet pepper
  • 2 cups ugwu (fluted pumpkin leaves)
  • 2 tablespoons crayfish
  • 2 pieces seasoning cubes
  • 1 teaspoons salt
  • 1.3 cups meat stock

Steps

  1. Cook the meat: Season 17.9 ounces assorted meat (beef, tripe, shaki) and 3.6 ounces stockfish with diced 1 pieces onion, 2 pieces seasoning cubes and 1 teaspoons salt. Cook until tender. Reserve the stock.
  2. Blend pepper: Blend 3 pieces scotch bonnet pepper with half of 1 pieces onion into a smooth paste. Set aside.
  3. Fry egusi: Heat 4 tablespoons palm oil in a pot. Mix 2 cups ground egusi (melon seeds) with a little water into a paste. Drop spoonfuls into the hot oil and fry for 5 minutes , stirring to break into chunks
  4. Add pepper and stock: Pour blended pepper into the pot. Add 1.3 cups meat stock, 2 tablespoons crayfish, and cooked meat. Stir well and cook for 15 minutes on medium heat.
  5. Add vegetables and serve: Stir in 2 cups ugwu (fluted pumpkin leaves), adjust seasoning with 1 teaspoons salt and 2 pieces seasoning cubes. Cook for 2 more minutes then remove from heat. Serve with swallow of choice.
